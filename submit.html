Creator Guide (Preview)

Purpose

This guide helps County staff propose AI agents that are useful, safe, and ready for ITD review. Agents should support staff work—not replace policy decisions or professional judgment.

Before you submit

  • Define one clear task the agent will support (keep scope narrow).
  • Confirm what data would be used and whether it is Public, Internal, or Confidential.
  • Design outputs so a person can review and edit before sharing or finalizing.

Appropriate examples

  • Drafting summaries, memos, and email templates from user-provided notes.
  • Turning bullet points into a structured report format.
  • Explaining existing County processes using approved references.

Not appropriate

  • Automated decision-making (e.g., eligibility, enforcement, approvals).
  • Resident-facing responses without human review.
  • Uploading or pasting sensitive/confidential information into tools that are not approved for it.

What ITD reviews

  • Data sources, sensitivity, and guardrails
  • Accuracy and usability validation
  • Privacy, security, and compliance alignment

Example Submissions

BAI Writer

Data sensitivity: Internal (user-provided text)

Purpose: Drafts clear summaries, memos, and email responses from bullet points staff provide.

Users: Administrative staff

Top tasks:

  • Turn notes into a clean memo format
  • Rewrite content for clarity and tone
  • Create response templates

Sample prompt: “Turn these bullet points into a 1-page memo for my manager. Use a professional tone.”

Field Inspection Assistant

Data sensitivity: Internal / Confidential (depends on content)

Purpose: Converts inspection notes into a structured report with consistent sections and follow-ups.

Users: Operations / inspection staff

Top tasks:

  • Format notes into a standardized inspection report
  • Extract action items and due dates
  • Create a follow-up checklist

Sample prompt: “Here are my inspection notes—format as a report and list recommended next steps.”

RFP Guide

Data sensitivity: Public / Internal (draft documents)

Purpose: Helps staff outline RFP sections, required language, and evaluation criteria templates.

Users: Guidance / procurement support

Top tasks:

  • Generate an RFP outline aligned to common County structure
  • Suggest evaluation criteria categories
  • Provide reusable prompt templates for consistent drafting

Sample prompt: “Create an RFP outline for [service] with sections and evaluation criteria.”

Examples are for guidance only. Submissions are reviewed by ITD before publication.
